Other DH Communities and Resources

Besides the associations, mentioned in a previous page, there are also a range of networks, infrastructures, and projects which contribute to develop DH.

  • DARIAH, the European Digital Research Infrastructure for the Arts and Humanities
  • Huma-Num, the French infrastructure for DH
  • CLARIN, the European Research Infrastructure for Language Resources and Technology
  • The Digital Humanities Course Registry developed by DARIAH and CLARIN
  • The NFDI (National Research Data Infrastructure) projects in Germany, including NFDI4Objects on material heritage; NFDI4Culture on material and immaterial cultural heritage; NFDI4Memory for history and memory institutions

Remember that most associations and projects are also active on social media (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn) where they share updates and jobs. Besides, there might be specific social media groups or hashtags. Examples from the cultural heritage sector are:

  • the Facebook groups:
    • Museum social media managers, with over 6600 members
    • Digital Archaeology Group, with over 3200 members
    • Digital history, mainly in German
    • Digital und Social Media im Museum, in German
  • Twitter hashtags:
    • #digitalhumanities - note that these are automatically retweeted by a bot
    • #globalDH, #DH
    • #twitterhistorians, #digiclass
    • by methods or technology, e.g. #dataviz, #LinkedData, #nlp, etc.
    • #musetech, #musedata, #musesocial
