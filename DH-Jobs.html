Finding a job in DH

This section includes links to websites where to look for DH jobs, and a couple of resources on the DH job market.
Job Market advice
  • The Academic Job Market Support Network (on Humanities Commons) includes a lot of sample cover letters, CVs, applications, and job market advice.
  • This post on cover letters by Brandon Walsh and Amanda Visconti offers a look at cover letters for DH jobs, with two sample letters annotated in details.
  • This is a useful collection of links and resources for a DH job search.
Academic jobs

These websites are academic jobs portals in general, including - of course - all types of academic jobs in DH.

  • EU academic jobs
  • UK academic jobs. You will find DH roles mostly under the 'Information Management and Librarianship' tag, but it is worth checking also under history and philosophy, languages and culture, and media and communication.
  • Dutch academic jobs. You will find DH roles under 'Language and culture'.
  • German, Swiss, Austrian academic jobs
  • Times Higher Education jobs portal.

Sometimes, DH jobs can also end up on mailing lists for other disciplines: for example, Chiara found her current job at ZIM on a website for cultural heritage academic jobs. So, it is worth considering also what 'traditional' discipline you see yourself most close to, and look at lists for that specific discipline.

Outside of academia

There are a range of possible career paths for DH specialists outside of academia, such as in heritage institutions or in industry. As a first example, this list of possible careers in 'Computing, technology and digital', from a website by the UK Government, shows the variety of paths that might be of interest. By clicking on each type of career, you will get a short overview: although most of it is focused on the UK context (e.g. pay, education), it might be interesting to explore the range of skills and the tasks entailed by these jobs.

The European Mu.Sa project investigated the emerging digital job profiles in museums and identified these roles:
  • Digital strategy manager: also known as Cultural ICT Consultant, Digital Cultural Manager, Cultural Digital Strategy Manager, Cultural ICT Ambassador, Digital cultural mediator, Cultural ICT Advisor, Cultural ICT specialist.   • Digital Collections Curator: also known as Digital Cultural Asset Manager, Digital Asset manager, Born-Digital Material Curator, Digital Curator This role-profile is specialised in preserving and managing born-digital materials. Develop online and offline exhibitions and content for other departments.
  • Digital Interactive Experience Developer: also known as Interactive Experience Developer, Digital Interactive Experience designer, Exhibit interactive designer. This role-profile is specialised in designing, developing and implementing innovative and interactive experiences providing a meaningful experience for all types of visitors.
  • Online Community Manager: also known as Online Cultural Community manager, Online community developer, Online community specialist, Social media specialist, Digital Media curator, Visual Media curator, New Media Manager, Digital Communication Manager, Social media manager.

Examples of commercial companies engaged with DH methods and offering DH services to institutions and researchers:

  • Huber Digital, a group of DH specialists supporting the publication and management of humanities research data
  • Facts & Files, developing tools for crowdsourcing in cultural institutions
  • Textloop, supporting the development of TEI projects (and other DH projects)
  • Fluxguide, developing apps and digial solutions for museums
  • 7reasons, specialised in 3D reconstructions, AR, VR, and interactive solutions for cultural heritage sites and institutions
  • Pausanio, digital cultural communication agency, offering services and training to museums
  • Anders & wissen, offering digital public history solutions
  • Progetti in Rete, offering XML Markup services, training, and assistance to cultural and research institutions
  • Digital Science: this is not specifically DH, but it is a consultancy which support digital research at large for universities, funders, and HE policy organisations.

Working as a freelance or for commercial companies (not only dedicated to humanities projects) are of course also other good options, and in this case a lot will depend on your own preferences, locations, skills.
