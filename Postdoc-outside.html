Careers outside of academia

A PhD can lead also to great research roles outside of academia, and the skills you will have developed during the PhD can be applied in a range of roles and sectors. Humanities PhD might work for research councils (e.g. the FWF in Austria); government; cultural institutions; or they can work as freelancers, for example contributing to research projects, writing reports, advising on methods and tools, etc. They can remain in universities and move to non-academic or support roles (e.g. grant writers, librarians, career development services). Many universities organise workshops on tranferrable skills, such as time management or writing skills, which are acquired during a PhD and can be valued by employers outside of academia.

Resources:

  • A good resource to get started is this post by FindAPhD, which demonstrates how to translate the PhD experience into skills for a CV targeted at employers outside of academia, and includes also a list of further links.
  • Targeted at historians: Beyond Academe, this is US-based (and dates to the early 2000s), but it contains a considerable list of resources, ideas, and links organised in a clearly way; some may be outdated, but in general this is a great starting point for historians (and nearby fields) considering possibilities in non-academic careers.
  • The career orientation tool by Euraxess offers a clear summary of the possibilities after a PhD. You can further explore them by using their Career Handbook (you can see it as a pdf or use it via the e-tool)

  • An example of transferrable skills PhDs will have is 20 Transferable Skills for PhDs by Cheeky Scientist, which has a lot of resources on careers outside of academia. It is US-focused and science-focused, but the transferable skills they list are largely applicable also to humanities PhD.
  • Versatile PhD has a list of possible career paths for humanities PhDs (note: you will need to register, for free, to access the full content).
  • Euraxess has also a section dedicated to jobs in outside of universities: this is the page for Austria, where you can see non-university research organisations and jobs in the public and private sector.
  • Trace was a project which tracked the careers of PhDs in Canada, and its website has many data, reflections, and interviews.
  • Career stories from the Netherlands: since most of the resources listed here come from the US/UK world, this link adds a perspective from the Netherlands.

Note that this page focuses in general on paths outside of academia for (mainly humanities) PhDs. Career paths specific for DH are explored in the DH section of the website.
